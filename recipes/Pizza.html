Pizza


Description


No pizza oven? No problem! Make your homemade pizza on the grill for that classic smoky, wood-fired flavor. The high heat creates a crisp crust with lightly charred edges, similar to what you would get from a traditional pizza oven. It is a great option for warm weather cooking and keeps the heat outside instead of in your kitchen. And even if you have a pizza oven, the grill is a great option when you want something a little easier but just as delicious.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  • 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
  • 1 pinch white sugar
  • 3 ⅓ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh basil

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. To make the dough: Pour warm water into a large bowl; dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let stand until yeast softens and begins to form a creamy foam, about 5 to 10 minutes.
  3. Mix in flour, 1 tablespoon olive oil, and salt until dough pulls away from the sides of the bowl.
  4. Turn onto a lightly floured surface. Knead until smooth, about 8 minutes.
  5. Place dough in a well-oiled bowl and cover with a damp cloth.
  6. Set aside to rise until doubled, about 1 hour. Punch down; knead in garlic and basil. Set aside to rise for 1 more hour, or until doubled again.
  7. Meanwhile, make the garlic oil: Combine 1/2 cup olive oil with minced garlic in a microwave-safe cup or bowl. Heat for 30 seconds in the microwave.
  8. Preheat an outdoor grill for high heat; brush the grate with garlic oil.
  9. Close the lid and cook until cheese melts. Remove from grill and set aside to cool for a few minutes. Repeat with second piece of dough.
